Hello! I'm Bruno,

a legislative advisor who loves exploring data. In my work at the Federal Senate of Brazil, I research a variety of topics to inform legislative proposals. I believe evidence-based public policy is the key to shape a better society. To get relevant insights for my work, I analyze data in R and Python. During my master, I worked at the Hertie School Data Science Lab, in Berlin. As a teaching assistant I offered consultations on R, data visualization and causal inference. In my free time, apart from exploring data, I like exploring new places and cultures. Reading, gaming, history and jogging are also my hobbies. In this page I present some of my projects and experiences. I am the creator of Coding the Past, a blog developed to teach data science to other social scientists.
